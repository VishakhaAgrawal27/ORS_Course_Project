Chart 12 · Driver Mechanics

Five drivers, three populations, one question.

Five rows of 3D ridges, one per MLR driver. In each row, three glowing curves stack along the depth axis: gray = pixels built in 2020, red = pixels the model adds by 2050, sage = pixels never urbanised. Y axis is the kernel density. Where the curves separate, the driver matters. Where they overlap, the driver is essentially noise.

Population (separates)
strong signal Built and new-by-2050 distributions sit firmly above mean (z > 0); never-built sits below. The sharpest separation across all five drivers.
Distance to roads
moderate signal Built and new sit at z < 0 (closer than average); never-built reaches z > 0. Visible split, narrower than population.
Distance to center
weak signal Built sits near z = 0; never-built bulges toward higher z (further out). Modest separation; the model relies less on this driver than expected.
Slope & elevation
no signal All three population curves overlap almost entirely. The Deccan plateau is geographically uniform; topography contributes negligibly to the model.
