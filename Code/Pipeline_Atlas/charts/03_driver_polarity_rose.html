Chart 03 · Driver Mechanics

Five drivers, five spokes.

Each radial spoke is one of the five MLR predictors. Spoke length = absolute value of the coefficient (how strongly the driver pushes built-up probability up or down). Color = sign: red for positive (more → more built), cyan for negative (more → less built). The visual punchline is population's dominance: it towers over every other driver. The MLR is essentially "where people are, urban grows" with three small geographic corrections.

Coefficient

Population (positive)
+1.71 By far the largest coefficient. The model says: the single best predictor of where a pixel will become urban is whether people already live nearby.
Distance to roads (negative)
-0.59 Second-largest. Pixels far from roads are much less likely to convert. The road network is the urbanisation skeleton.
Distance to center (negative)
-0.20 Smaller. Center proximity matters but only modestly: Hyderabad's growth is not radial; it follows roads more than radial distance.
Slope & elevation (small +)
+0.06 & +0.05 Both positive and very small. The Deccan plateau is uniformly flat; topography is essentially noise.
