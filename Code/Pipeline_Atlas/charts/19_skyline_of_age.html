Chart 19 · Spatial Reveals

Hyderabad as a city of stacked decades.

Every built-up pixel becomes a tower. Tower height encodes how long the pixel has been built: the 2020 baseline (oldest) rises tallest in dark gray; each subsequent five-year cohort drops a notch and shifts toward forest green. New cohorts emit red embers rising into the night, fading as they age. The result is a stepped pyramid centered on the historic core, with younger fringes flattening outward.

Old core, new fringe
7× The 2020 baseline towers are 7× taller than the 2050 newcomers. The simulation visibly stratifies the city by tenure: old, established centre vs raw new periphery.
Shape signature
Stepped pyramid Newer (shorter) cohorts settle around the perimeter of older clusters. The model rarely adds isolated outliers — its neighborhood weight pulls all new growth into existing built clusters.
Growth velocity
+104% Built-up area more than doubles over 30 years, but spatial extent grows only ~30% — the new growth is dense in-fill of already-developing zones, not greenfield sprawl.
